# eMFer Launch Content - LinkedIn

## Option 1: Main Launch Post

I am excited to share something I have been building: **eMFer - Empirical Mutual Fund Evaluation & Research**.

I started my investing journey in 2018 without much guidance. Like many first-time investors, I made mistakes, fumbled through information, and slowly learned how to think about mutual fund performance beyond simple return numbers.

Over time, I wished there was one simple research workspace that could help me answer questions like:

- Has this fund performed consistently across market cycles?
- How volatile has the journey been?
- How does this fund compare with its peers?
- What would a SIP or lumpsum investment have looked like historically?
- Is the return worth the risk taken?

That thought became **eMFer**.

eMFer helps investors explore mutual funds using:

- historical NAV trends
- rolling CAGR analysis
- performance consistency
- risk-return comparison
- SIP and lumpsum return outcomes
- fund comparison dashboards
- eMFer Funds Marathon
- Scout, an AI research assistant for simpler explanations

The goal is not to give buy or sell recommendations.

The goal is to make mutual fund research easier to understand, especially for beginner and intermediate investors who want to make more informed, performance-based decisions.

This is still an early version, and I would genuinely appreciate feedback from investors, finance folks, product builders, and anyone curious about data-driven investing.

## Option 3: Product-Led LinkedIn Post

Most mutual fund platforms show point-to-point returns.

But a fund's journey matters too.

Two funds can have similar 5-year returns, but very different experiences along the way. One may be more consistent. One may be more volatile. One may recover better after difficult periods.

That is the idea behind **eMFer - Empirical Mutual Fund Evaluation & Research**.

eMFer helps users study mutual funds through rolling returns, risk-return analysis, comparison dashboards, SIP/lumpsum outcomes, and an AI assistant called Scout.

It is built for investors who want to go beyond headline returns and understand performance more deeply.
